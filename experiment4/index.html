Description

What are you trying to acomplish with this experiment?
This is an infinite Where's Waldo generator. It is a casual clicking game. There is variation in the environment with beaches, oceans, dirt, and grassy regions. There is also a lot of variation in people with several skin colors and over 10 clothes colors. Some people spawn with hats (Waldos). People do not spawn in water.“Waldo Tiles” are clickable. When the user clicks on a person with a hat, the tile turns red and the score is increased.


Technical

Describe in detail what you did technically and how it works?
I used perlin noise to generate biomes with dirt, sand, grass, and oceans. I also used noise to generate variation in the clothing and skin tones of the people. I used object mapping to decipher and keep track of Waldo tiles, in order to update the global count, and change the tiles to red.


Reflection

I am highly satisfied with this infinite generator because I was able to gamify it. I think that it looks pretty good too for p5js graphics. If I were to expand upon it, I would probably move away from p5 graphics and try to incorporate my own art.


Statement


INFINITY
I made an infinite Where's Waldo generator. It is a casual clicking game. There is variation in the environment with beaches, oceans, dirt, and grassy regions. There is also a lot of variation in people with several skin colors and over 10 clothes colors. Some people spawn with hats (Waldos). People do not spawn in water.“Waldo Tiles” are clickable. When the user clicks on a person with a hat, the tile turns red and the score is increased. The world expands up, down, left, and right infinitely.
VARIATION IN SPACE
There is variation in the environment with beaches, oceans, dirt, and grassy regions. There is also a lot of variation in people with several skin colors and over 10 clothes colors. Some people spawn with hats (Waldos). People do not spawn in water.
VARIATION IN KEY
Different noise generates significantly different terrain to make entirely new worlds and Waldo locations.
SIGNS OF LIFE
“Waldo Tiles” are clickable. When the user clicks on a person with a hat, the tile turns red and the score is increased. This progresses time within the game by how many Waldos have been discovered as tiles get marked red and the count increases. Yes, a change in game state/progression does count as a progression in time. If I really wanted to, I could have the water wiggle and I even have the code for it (see https://infinite-shire.glitch.me/ a generator I stopped working on), but I believe that takes away from the game, and as the artistic creator I say that progression in game, is progression in world time.
